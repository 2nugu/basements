# Basements — Phase Roadmap

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-13  
**Author cadence:** 1 person, part-time. All time estimates are *calendar* (not
intensive full-time) — multiply by 0.3× if working full-time.

This roadmap replaces the previous "From Earthworms to Galaxies" framing with a
**concrete, sequenced milestone plan** scoped to what a single engineer can
realistically ship while preserving paper-track viability.

---

## Long-term direction (reframe)

**Niche:** Open MPM ↔ Rigid coupling benchmark suite for **robotics in granular
media** (sand, snow, regolith, agricultural soil).

**Not:** A general-purpose physics engine alternative to Bullet/MuJoCo/Genesis.
Those are 10+ person-year products; competing head-on is unrealistic and
distracts from the actual contribution.

**Audience priority** (Pivot 5 — agricultural robotics identity):
1. **Agricultural robotics researchers** — autonomous tractors,
   precision seeders, robotic cultivators, soil-compaction prediction.
   Primary venue: Biosystems Engineering / Computers and Electronics
   in Agriculture.
2. **Soil-machine interaction / terramechanics community** — tine
   draft prediction, traction modelling beyond empirical Bekker fits.
   Primary venue: Journal of Terramechanics.
3. **Field robotics community** — rover-on-regolith analogues
   (ICRA/IROS agricultural workshops).
4. Graphics / SCA reviewers comparing coupling schemes (deprioritised
   under Pivot 5; alternative venue only).
5. Open-source contributors who want a clean Taichi-alternative
   coupling kernel.

### Milestone 2.4 — Constitutive Model Extension (Paper #2 origin)

**Why:** Round 14's `foot_step` results showed rebound coefficient
e = 0 across *every* coupler — Finding 5 of Paper #1. The cause is in
the constitutive model, not the coupler: our MPM sand uses elastic-only
Drucker-Prager plasticity with no rate-dependent elastic recovery. No
amount of coupler refinement (M0..M4) can produce realistic rebound
under that constitutive choice.

Paper #1 *isolates* this finding and hands it off; Paper #2 *fixes* it.
The clean Paper-#1/Paper-#2 split lets each paper carry a single
contribution at full strength.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Add a hyperelastic energy density to `MPMSolver::apply_plasticity`.
      Two candidates:
      - Fixed-corotated: ψ(F) = μ·∥F − R∥² + (λ/2)·(J − 1)², where R is
        the rotation factor of F's polar decomposition. Cheap (one SVD,
        already in our pipeline) and well-behaved.
      - Neo-Hookean: ψ(F) = (μ/2)·(tr(FᵀF) − 3) − μ·ln(J) + (λ/2)·(ln J)².
        Standard graphics choice; slightly stiffer for small strains.
- [ ] Rate-dependent recovery: optional viscoelastic time constant on
      the deviatoric branch. Knob: τ_recovery → 0 means snap-back,
      τ_recovery → ∞ means pure plasticity (our current behaviour).
- [ ] Re-run `foot_step` after the constitutive change. **Paper #2's
      headline figure** = rebound coefficient `e` vs τ_recovery sweep,
      showing dry sand ≈ 0.05–0.15 lands on the expected branch.
- [ ] Calibration against published terramechanics data — Bekker /
      Wong dry-sand parameters become quantitatively meaningful.

**Paper #2 working title (placeholder):**

> _Hyperelastic Constitutive Extension for Granular MPM: Restoring
>  Rebound and Calibratable Terramechanics in a Drucker-Prager Solver_

**Phase 2 exit criterion:** full paper submitted to SCA / EG full track
**AND** Paper #2 draft begun with the constitutive change verified on
`foot_step`.

### Why this re-ordered 3-paper series (Pivot 6)

- **Real algorithmic novelty at every paper.** Paper #2 (differentiable
  + adjoint method for plastic projection) is genuinely first-in-class
  for MPM-rigid coupling; paper #3 (GPU + adaptive resolution for
  real-time differentiable MPM) is system + algorithm; paper #4
  (field deployment) demonstrates the full stack.
- **Identity coherence.** All three papers serve the KNU Smart
  Agriculture + Biosystems Engineering identity: agricultural
  robotics policy learning, real-time field control, deployment in
  Korean fields.
- **Reviewer fatigue defence.** Each paper has bounded scope and
  cites the previous; future-work direction is the next paper.
- **Risk distribution.** Paper #2 reject doesn't sink paper #3 (which
  is system / engineering layer, different review pool); paper #4 is
  application-paper review pool, fully orthogonal.
- **Community formation absorbed into paper #2 §6.** External
  validation protocol (paper #1 Appendix C) remains operational; lab
  submissions are part of paper #2's validation, not a separate
  community paper.

## Cross-phase rules

1. **Each phase produces ≥ 1 deliverable that survives even if later phases
   slip** (paper, figure, public library version).
2. **No new top-level feature without a regression test.**
3. **At any phase exit, repo is buildable + 100% test passing** (currently 84/84).
4. **Documentation registered in PROJECT_STATUS.md** when added.
5. **Pivot decision points** (phase exits) are explicit checkpoints —
   continue, fork, or stop, but never drift.
